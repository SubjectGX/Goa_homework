“Red Constructor” is a Russian documentary film directed by Andrey I and animator Armen Petrosyan. The script is based on Thomas Mann's novel “The Magic Mountain” (1924),
coupled with the idea of ​​​​creating artificial people in the USSR (in the 1940-1950s).
The film uses authentic film documentation from the period 1938-1980, but it is given an extremely free interpretation.

info

The film is divided into three chapters and has two storylines, one of which is fiction and the other is documentary.
In the artistic line, the visual series consists mainly of the same type of frames with hallucinatory content: a red room, a girl in red silk, located in a picture frame, kaleidoscopic images.
A female voice-over reads an excerpt from “The Magic Mountain,” in which the thoughts of a deceased soldier are heard (his consciousness still resides in a dead body). There is a reflection on Christ and the Apostles and the interpretation of the word “neighbor/twin”, which occurs in one of the biblical episodes. The soldier's body then becomes a victim of necrophiliac children. The documentary part of the film consists of natural filming of operations to transplant toes onto a human hand; short moments from the life of conjoined twins; clippings, possibly a propaganda film for doctors on the use of fibrinolyzed blood in practical medicine, etc.
